The Sweetday Rule 🍩

One Sweetday a week. Make it worth the wait.

The Idea

When is the last time you feel excited about having a treat?

The Sweetday Rule keeps the magic alive by treating sugar like a VIP guest instead of background noise.

Skip desserts for six days, and save your "golden ticket" for one epic day of indulgence.

Why Bother?

It's not about losing weight or being "healthy." It's entirely about the payoff.

  • Keep the magic: When you have a cookie every single day, it stops being special. Waiting for your Sweetday turns the spark back on.
  • The "Win": You put in the week, you earn the reward. It makes the dessert feel like an actual win.
  • Zero guilt: Since this is a planned event, you can just eat it and enjoy it. No "cheating," no regret. Just a scheduled payout.

What counts as "Sweet"?

The "Sweetday" Stuff (The Rewards)

These are the heavy hitters you're saving:

  • Candies & Desserts: Chocolate bars, gummies, hard candies, ice cream, pudding.
  • Bakery: Donuts, cookies, brownies, cake, sweet croissants.
  • Drinks: Soda, boba, sugary lattes, energy drinks, fruit juice.

The "Everyday" Stuff (The Staples)

These stay in your rotation all week because they're just fuel:

  • Real food: Rice, oats, bread, potatoes, pasta, beans, vegetables, meats, fish, eggs, tofu. This is the stuff that actually keeps you going.
  • Whole Fruit: Apples, berries, bananas. It has fiber, so it doesn't hit your system the same way as processed sugar.
  • Drinks: Black coffee, tea, plain or sparkling water.

FAQ

Is it really sustainable?

I've been doing this since 2020. It works because it's just a way to make sure the stuff I actually like stays fun. Because it's focused on enjoyment rather than restriction, it's been incredibly easy to stick with long-term.

Did you lose weight?

Yes, but only for the first few months. My weight has stayed the same for years since then.

Will you binge eat?

It didn't happen to myself. Because the "Sweetday" is a planned, guilt-free event, you don't feel the need to overdo it. When you feel satisfied and have had enough, you just stop.

What about sauces that contain sugar, like ketchup?

A little bit of sugar in a savory sauce is fine to include as part of your everyday meals, as long as it's not a "sweet" dish.

What about zero-sugar sodas like Coke Zero?

These go in the "Sweetday" category. Even though they have no sugar, they are designed to mimic a sweet profile, which keeps the "crave" active during the week.

Can I have smoothies?

It depends on how they are made. If you make it yourself at home (e.g., banana, kale, milk), it's fuel—you control the ingredients. If it's a commercial smoothie from a cafe or organic shop, it's a reward, as these are often sweetened with extra fruit concentrates or syrups.

What about cough syrup or vitamin gummies?

These are functional, not treats. They don't count toward the rule.
